What is the Grothendieck construction? And why is it important? I'd be lying if I said I fully understood the significance of Grothendieck fibrations and whatever, but for our purposes, it's useful for collecting data together in a bunch of different categories. If you have categories that are "indexed" over a variable (another category in this case), then the Grothendieck construction is kind of like taking the union $$\cup_i C_i$$ of the categories over the index.

Really important for us: certain Grothendieck constructions describe lenses! And those show up all over the place, such as in databases and dynamical systems.
They also describe the forward maps, that is trajectories and behaviors, in David's systems theory.

In other descriptions online, you'll find the construction defined either covariantly or contravariantly, with the other being described as the dual. We'll go over both in detail visually. Further, lenses contain a pointwise opposite in the integrand, so we'll visualize 4 combinations in total.
